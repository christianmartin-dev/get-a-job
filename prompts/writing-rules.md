# Writing Rules

Apply these rules to all generated text. Cover letters, recruiter responses, negotiation scripts, and any prose output must pass these checks before delivery.

Sources: /humanizer, /stop-slop, /recruiter-comms voice rules.

## Banned vocabulary

Delete on sight. No exceptions.

**AI vocabulary:** delve, crucial, robust, comprehensive, nuanced, foster, enhance, leverage, synergy, landscape (abstract), tapestry (abstract), testament, underscore (verb), showcase, pivotal, groundbreaking, vibrant, nestled, interplay, intricate, garner, crucial, additionally (as sentence opener), align with, enduring, valuable

**AI intensifiers:** deeply, truly, fundamentally, inherently, simply, literally, inevitably

**Openers:** "I am writing to express my interest," "I am passionate about," "In today's rapidly evolving," "I hope this helps," "please don't hesitate," "I'd be happy to," "Here's the thing:," "The uncomfortable truth is," "It turns out," "Let me be clear," "Can we talk about"

**Emphasis crutches:** "Full stop." / "Period.," "Let that sink in.," "This matters because," "Make no mistake," "Here's why that matters"

**Tone words:** excited, thrilled, delighted, happy to help, game-changer

**Business jargon:** navigate (challenges), unpack (analysis), lean into, double down, deep dive, circle back, moving forward, on the same page, take a step back

## Structural rules

**No em dashes.** Use commas or periods. Every time.

**No exclamation marks** in professional correspondence.

**No rule of three.** Two items beat three. If you have three parallel items, cut one or combine two.

**No binary contrasts.** "Not because X. Because Y." is a telegraphed reversal. State Y directly.

**No dramatic fragmentation.** "[Noun]. That's it. That's the [thing]." is performative simplicity. Use complete sentences.

**No negative parallelisms.** "It's not just X; it's Y" is overused. Say what it is.

**No rhetorical setups.** "What if I told you..." and "Here's what I mean:" announce insight instead of delivering it. Make the point.

**No superficial -ing phrases** tacked onto sentences. "highlighting the importance of..." and "underscoring its significance..." add fake depth.

**No synonym cycling.** Don't vary terms for the same thing just to avoid repetition. Repetition is fine.

**No meta-commentary.** "Hint:," "Plot twist:," "Spoiler:" have no place in professional writing.

## Positive rules

**Vary sentence length.** Short then long. Two shorts then a long. Never three sentences the same length in a row.

**Be specific.** Name the technology, reference their job posting language, cite numbers. "Reduced API latency by 40%" beats "improved performance significantly."

**Use simple constructions.** "is" and "are" and "has" over "serves as" and "stands as" and "boasts."

**Lead with substance.** First sentence should contain information, not a greeting or throat-clearing.

**One "interested" per document max.** Never "excited" in professional correspondence.

**Match their tone.** Read the job posting or company website. Mirror their level of formality. LinkedIn messages get casual professional. Email from a VP gets slightly more formal.

**Trust the reader.** State facts directly. Skip softening, justification, hand-holding. No "And that's okay." No "I promise."

## Quality check

Before delivering any generated text, score 1-10 on each:

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Directness | Statements or announcements? |
| Rhythm | Varied or metronomic? |
| Trust | Respects reader intelligence? |
| Authenticity | Sounds human? |
| Density | Anything cuttable? |

Below 35/50: revise before presenting.
